Sikar vs Kota for NEET Preparation: An Honest Comparison

For two decades "medical coaching" meant Kota. That's no longer automatic. Sikar has emerged as the value alternative — lower fees, lower living costs, smaller batches — and its results now compete at the very top: a Sikar institute produced a perfect-score NEET AIR 1 in 2024.

Where Kota still wins

Scale. The biggest brands, the deepest test-series ecosystems, the largest peer pool. If a student thrives on competition density — being surrounded by thousands of serious aspirants — Kota's environment is unmatched. Its institutional machinery (question banks, faculty benches, result-analytics) is the product of thirty years of iteration.

Where Sikar wins

Cost and closeness. Fees and living expenses run meaningfully below Kota's. Batches tend to be smaller, which means doubt-solving actually happens for average students, not only toppers. The environment is lower-pressure — a real factor, given how openly the coaching industry now discusses student stress. And the results gap has closed: institutes like Gurukripa, PCP and Navjeevan post selections every year, with PCP's 720/720 AIR 1 in 2024 the headline proof.

How to decide

Be honest about the student, not the city. Self-driven students who feed off competition tend to extract more from Kota. Students who benefit from supervision, smaller classes and a calmer environment often do better in Sikar — and the family budget stretches further. If at all possible, visit both cities before enrolling: sit in one class in each, walk the hostel areas, and talk to current students outside the campus gate. Two days of visits beats two months of online debate.
