Sikar's transformation into a national NEET hub stopped being a local story in 2024, when Devesh Joshi, a classroom student who lived in the hostel at the city's PCP (Prince Career Pioneer), scored a perfect 720/720 in NEET UG to take All India Rank 1 — a result documented by PCP itself, with additional detail carried in a PCP-sourced profile syndicated via The Tribune (labelled there as a sponsored feature, not independent reporting). Two more PCP students, Rohan Sharma and Sajid Khan, scored 710/720 the same year. Families now weigh Sikar seriously against Kota — usually at meaningfully lower cost — and the city's roughly three-decade coaching history, referenced in the same coverage, means it's no longer a fallback option. Here is how Sikar's NEET institutes actually compare, using the ratings and facts on our own listing pages, plus what to check beyond the star rating.

What the ratings say

On our platform, the most-reviewed NEET institute in Sikar is Navjeevan NEET Academy, a verified listing holding a 5.0 rating across 59 student reviews — the largest review base of any institute in the city. Gurukripa Career Institute, running since 2007, is also verified with a 5.0 from 23 reviews, and remains the city's most established full-scale player, with foundation courses, hostels and a scholarship test (GSAT) offering large fee waivers. Matrix rates 4.7 from 20 reviews, and the newer Path Career Institute holds a 5.0 from 19 early reviews — a strong start, though the smaller sample means it's worth watching how the rating holds as more students review it.

The established names

PCP, the institute behind the 2024 AIR 1, has operated since 2009 and rates 4.2 from 10 reviews with us — a smaller review base than its results might suggest, which is exactly why a personal visit matters more than the number. CLC is the city's oldest NEET/JEE institute, teaching since 1996, and rates 4.5 from 11 reviews. National brands have arrived too: ALLEN and Sri Chaitanya both opened Sikar campuses in recent years, though their local review bases are still small, since they're newer to this specific city even if their brand history elsewhere is long.

Why Sikar's rise matters beyond one result

A single AIR 1 doesn't make a coaching ecosystem — but a cluster of consistent selections across several institutes does, and that's what's built up in Sikar over the past decade. Lower fees than Kota, lower cost of living, and generally smaller batch sizes have combined to draw families who previously wouldn't have considered anywhere but Kota or Delhi. Our companion piece, Sikar vs Kota for NEET preparation, goes deeper into exactly where each city still has the edge.

How to choose between them

Treat ratings as a screen, not a verdict

Especially where review counts are small — a 5.0 from 10 reviews and a 4.7 from 20 reviews are closer in reliability than the headline numbers suggest. Shortlist two or three institutes based on the table above, then move to an in-person check.

Ask three questions on your visit

Who specifically will teach your batch, per subject, and for how long have they taught here? What is the actual batch size, not the marketing number? And how, mechanically, do doubt sessions work — is there a fixed daily slot, or does it depend on catching a teacher after class?

Check accommodation logistics on the same trip

Most institutes cluster around Piprali Road, and typical room plans in the area run roughly ₹8,000–15,000 a month. Sorting out where you'll stay on the same trip as your institute visit saves a second journey and lets you judge the actual commute time, not the map distance.

What a review count actually tells you

A rating built on 59 reviews, like Navjeevan's, carries more statistical weight than a rating built on 10, even if the 10-review institute's average is technically higher. This doesn't mean the smaller-sample institute is worse — plenty of genuinely excellent institutes are simply newer to collecting reviews on any platform — but it does mean a wider shortlist visit matters more when review counts are thin. Treat any institute with fewer than 15–20 reviews as "promising but unconfirmed" rather than "proven," regardless of how high the average looks.

Questions that separate a strong institute from a strong marketing budget

Ask about the actual selection ratio, not just the headline topper

A single AIR 1 or AIR-adjacent result says less about an institute's overall effectiveness than its selection ratio — the proportion of a full batch that clears NEET at all, and how many clear it comfortably above a competitive rank. Institutes with genuinely strong batches are usually willing to share this number; institutes leaning entirely on one headline result sometimes aren't.

Ask how doubt-clearing scales during peak exam-prep months

Doubt support that works well in month two, when batches are still settling in, can break down in month ten when every student has urgent, exam-adjacent questions at once. Ask specifically how the institute handles this seasonal spike, not just how it works on a quiet week.

Ask what happens if a student needs to repeat a year

NEET has a meaningfully high repeat-attempt rate nationally, and how an institute treats a repeating student — fee structure, batch placement, whether the approach changes at all for a second attempt — is a genuine signal of whether it's built around student outcomes or around first-year enrolment numbers.

How Sikar's institutes typically structure a NEET batch

Most of the established names on our listings run a similar broad structure: a foundation phase covering NCERT-level fundamentals across Physics, Chemistry and Biology, followed by an integrated phase combining new topics with cumulative revision, and a final months-long phase dominated by full-length tests and targeted repair of weak areas. Where institutes genuinely differ is in the ratio of teaching time to test time in that middle phase, and in how personalised the repair work gets once test data starts accumulating — precisely the kind of detail that a rating alone can't show you, and that's worth asking about directly on a visit.

Beyond ratings: what to physically check on a Sikar visit

Rating aside, a short in-person visit answers questions no listing page can. Walk the actual route from the institute to the hostels you're considering at the time you'd realistically commute, not at midday. Ask to see a recent, dated test-series scorecard from an actual batch, not a curated highlight. And confirm directly, in writing, what the all-in annual cost looks like once hostel, mess, study material and any test-series fees are added to the headline coaching fee — a detail our general institute-selection checklist covers for any city, not just Sikar.

Frequently asked questions

Is Sikar as good as Kota for NEET now?

It depends on the student. See our detailed comparison, Sikar vs Kota for NEET Preparation, for where each city genuinely wins.

How much does NEET coaching cost in Sikar compared to Kota?

Sikar's institutes generally run lower fees than Kota's largest brands, though exact pricing varies by institute and batch — always confirm the all-in fee directly, as our institute-selection checklist recommends.

Do I need to move to Sikar, or can I prepare from home?

Most serious NEET aspirants in Sikar's institutes are hostel residents, since the batch structure and daily test cycle are built around being on campus. If you're relocating, our guide on moving to Sikar for coaching covers the practical planning.

How reliable is a single national topper result as a signal of institute quality?

Useful as one data point, but not sufficient on its own — a strong selection ratio across the full batch, not one exceptional student, is the better long-term signal of consistent teaching quality.

What's the biggest mistake families make when choosing between Sikar institutes?

Relying solely on the star rating without checking review count context, and skipping the in-person visit entirely on the assumption that a high rating alone is proof enough.

Do newer Sikar institutes need more time to prove themselves before being trustworthy?

Not necessarily more time so much as more reviews and a clearer selection-ratio track record — a newer institute with strong early reviews and transparent results can be a reasonable choice, provided the family applies the same in-person verification described above rather than relying on the rating alone.

How often should this comparison of Sikar institutes be treated as current?

Ratings and review counts shift meaningfully year to year as new batches graduate and new reviews come in, so treat any specific numbers here as a snapshot — always check the live rating and review count on our Sikar coaching listings page before making a final decision, rather than relying solely on the figures quoted in this article.
