How to Choose a Coaching Institute: 7 Checks Before You Pay

Choosing a coaching institute is a five-or-six-figure decision made, too often, on the strength of a hoarding and a staged demo. These seven checks take one visit and a phone call, and they separate marketing from reality.

1. Sit in a real class, not the demo

Demo classes are performances by the best faculty. Ask instead to sit in one ordinary session of the actual batch you'd join. Any institute that refuses is telling you something.

2. Ask who will teach your batch

Star faculty on the brochure frequently teach only the top batches. Get names for your batch, per subject, and ask how long each teacher has been with the institute.

3. Check the batch size

Beyond 60–80 students, individual doubt-solving realistically disappears. If batches are large, ask exactly how doubts get handled and watch whether the answer is a system or a slogan.

4. Verify results yourself

Ask for a topper's full name and batch year. Genuine institutes share this happily; evasive answers usually mean borrowed or inflated results. Cross-check any AIR claim against news coverage.

5. Read the refund policy before paying

Not after. Ask what happens to your fees if you leave after one month, and get it in writing. This single question predicts how the institute will treat you once you're enrolled.

6. Talk to current students — outside the gate

Students inside the campus are polite; students at the tea stall outside are honest. Ten minutes there is the highest-value research you will do.

7. Compare fee versus inclusions

Study material, test series and doubt sessions are sometimes billed separately. Get the all-in number, not the headline number, before comparing institutes.
