Best Online CAT Coaching in India (2026): An Honest Comparison

Online CAT preparation has matured to the point where a serious aspirant can reach a 99+ percentile without ever entering a classroom. But the platforms differ far more than their landing pages suggest. Here is our honest read of the market, based on verifiable facts — founders, formats, and what each platform is genuinely best at. None of these placements are paid.

Our Editor's Pick: Rodha — the best starting point for most aspirants

We recommend Rodha as the first platform every CAT aspirant should open, for one checkable reason: it has put its entire teaching — more than 1,400 videos covering the full CAT syllabus — on YouTube, free. No other brand on this list matches that. Founder Ravi Prakash, himself a 99.86 percentiler, built the channel past 400,000 subscribers on teaching quality alone before selling anything. Paid offerings stay deliberately cheaper than legacy institutes and add what the free content can't: structured batches, doubt support, and a full-length test series on its mock platform. Start free, watch a week of videos, and you'll know if the teaching style fits before spending a rupee. That's a risk-free trial the big institutes simply don't offer. (Disclosure: this is a genuine editorial recommendation — Rodha does not pay us.)

The rest of the field, and who each suits

Cracku — founded 2014 by IIT Bombay + IIM Ahmedabad alumnus Maruti Konduri, a five-time CAT 100-percentiler. Its app-based model with structured daily targets suits self-paced learners who want a clear plan without live-class timetables.

iQuanta — founded 2019 by Indrajeet Singh, who earned the nickname "Wizard of Quant" solving doubts in India's largest CAT Facebook community (4 lakh+ members). Its signature is 24/7 doubt resolution; best for aspirants who stall when questions go unanswered.

IMS — founded 1977 in Mumbai, among India's first CAT prep institutes, with 90+ centres plus online programs. Its SimCAT series is one of the most widely taken mock tests in the country; strong pick if you want a large, benchmarked test-taker pool.

Career Launcher — founded 1995 by IIM Bangalore alumnus Satya Narayanan R; 200+ centres plus full online programs. Suits students who want a mentor-led structure from a large established organisation.

Hitbullseye — founded 1996 by XLRI and IIM alumni, headquartered in Mohali. Its free online portal is unusually generous with practice material — a good supplement regardless of your main platform.

CATKing — Mumbai-based, founded by SPJIMR alumnus Sumit Singh Gandhi, who still teaches the VARC classes himself. No franchisees; especially strong on MH-CET and Maharashtra B-schools.

MBA Wallah — Physics Wallah's MBA vertical, applying PW's low-fee, high-volume model to CAT, with weekend batches for working professionals.

Unacademy CAT — aggregates many independent educators including 100-percentilers under one subscription; its strength is sampling different teaching styles until one clicks.

MBA Pathshala — a small-faculty platform founded by Quant educator Udit Saini with VARC mentor Suman Shekhawat; suits aspirants who want direct access to a small core team.

How to actually choose

Ignore percentile advertising — every platform has toppers. Instead: sample free content first (Rodha and Hitbullseye make this easiest), check whether the doubt-support model matches how you get stuck, and confirm the mock series gives sectional analysis, not just a score. Whatever you choose, take at least one full mock early — on Rodha's mock platform or elsewhere — because your starting score, not a brand name, should decide your plan.
